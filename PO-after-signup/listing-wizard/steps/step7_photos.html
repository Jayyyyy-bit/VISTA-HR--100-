How to capture a 360° virtual tour

Free using the Google Street View app on your phone

Step 1 of 5
1

Download Google Street View

Install the Google Street View app on your smartphone — it's completely free.

Google Play (Android) App Store (iPhone)
Already have it? You can skip to the next step.
2

Stand in the center of the room

Go inside the room you want to capture. Stand as close to the center of the room as possible for the best panorama coverage.

Turn on all the lights
Open curtains/windows for natural light
Remove clutter and personal items
Make the bed and tidy up first
3

Capture the 360° panorama

Open Google Street View, tap the orange camera button, then select "Take a Photo Sphere."

a Tap the + button at the bottom
b Select "Photo Sphere"
c Follow the orange dot — aim your phone at each dot until it turns blue
d Cover all directions: left, right, up, down, forward, behind
Takes about 1–2 minutes per room. Hold your phone steady for each shot.
4

Export & save the panorama

After capturing, Google Street View will stitch all photos together automatically into a 360° equirectangular image.

a Go to Private tab in Street View
b Tap your new Photo Sphere → tap the 3 dots menu
c Select "Export photo" or "Save to device"
d The saved image will be in your phone's Gallery / Photos
The exported file is the equirectangular panorama — it will look stretched/distorted when viewed as a regular photo. That's normal!
5

Upload to VISTA-HR

Transfer the panorama image to your computer, then upload it here using the upload button above.

File size: ideally under 20MB
Format: JPG or PNG
Minimum resolution: 4000 × 2000px
Once uploaded, residents can do a full 360° walkthrough of your room from the VISTA-HR listing page!
